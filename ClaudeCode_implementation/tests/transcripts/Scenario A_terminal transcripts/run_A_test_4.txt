# Business Requirement Document (BRD)  
**Project:** NovaMart BI Solution for Customer Interactions and Sales Performance Analysis  
**Prepared for:** Marketing and Sales Analysts, NovaMart  
**Purpose:** Define the business requirements for a BI solution based on a created DWH connected to Power BI for weekly ad-hoc analysis.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

NovaMart requires a BI solution that provides a single, clean analytical source of information for a team of 5 marketing and sales analysts. The current process relies on Excel tables and manual SQL queries on raw CSV exports, which consumes significant time each week. The new solution will be based on a created data warehouse (DWH) and connected to Power BI to support weekly ad-hoc analysis and dashboarding.

The solution must consolidate customer, product, and interaction data from three CSV sources into a reliable analytical layer. It will enable analysts to answer key commercial questions about revenue, order value, discount and promo usage, conversion behavior, customer segmentation, and review ratings.

---

## 2. Business Background and Problem Statement

NovaMart’s analysts currently work with raw CSV exports using Excel and manual SQL queries. This approach is time-consuming, repetitive, and makes it difficult to maintain a consistent view of commercial performance.

The business needs a centralized analytical source that:
- reduces manual weekly reporting effort,
- standardizes key metrics,
- supports ad-hoc analysis,
- and enables Power BI dashboards for operational decision-making.

The BI solution will be used by business-oriented analysts with basic to intermediate technical skills. It must therefore be easy to consume, consistent, and structured for self-service analysis.

---

## 3. Business Goals and Objectives

### 3.1 Business Goals
1. Provide a single, clean analytical source of truth for commercial analysis.
2. Reduce the time analysts spend on manual reporting and SQL extraction.
3. Enable weekly analysis of sales performance, customer behavior, and product interaction trends.
4. Support Power BI dashboards for recurring and ad-hoc business questions.

### 3.2 Objectives
- Consolidate the three CSV sources into a DWH.
- Standardize business definitions for core KPIs.
- Make weekly operational analysis faster and more reliable.
- Enable segmentation by category, season, subscription status, geography, and customer behavior.
- Support analysis of product interaction funnel performance.

### 3.3 Business Goals Linked to Requirements
- **Goal 1:** Single analytical source of truth  
  - Supported by requirements for integrated DWH tables and standardized KPI definitions.
- **Goal 2:** Reduce manual reporting effort  
  - Supported by weekly batch refresh and Power BI-ready analytical structures.
- **Goal 3:** Improve weekly commercial analysis  
  - Supported by KPI and dimensional analysis requirements.
- **Goal 4:** Enable self-service dashboards  
  - Supported by curated data model and business-friendly granularity.

## 6. KPI Definitions

### 6.1 Total Revenue
- **Definition:** `SUM(Purchase Amount USD)`
- **Granularity:** category, season, subscription status, week

### 6.2 Average Order Value
- **Definition:** `Total Revenue / number of transactions`
- **Granularity:** category, subscription status

### 6.3 Discount Rate
- **Definition:** `COUNT(transactions where Discount Applied = Yes) / COUNT(all transactions)`
- **Granularity:** category, season

### 6.4 Promo Code Redemption Rate
- **Definition:** `COUNT(transactions where Promo Code Used = Yes) / COUNT(all transactions)`
- **Granularity:** subscription status, season

### 6.5 Product Conversion Rate
- **Definition:** `COUNT(interaction events where type = purchase) / COUNT(interaction events where type = view)`
- **Granularity:** category

### 6.6 Customer Purchase Frequency Distribution
- **Definition:** Distribution of customers grouped by `Frequency of Purchases`
- **Granularity:** snapshot segmentation, no specific time granularity

### 6.7 Average Review Rating
- **Definition:** `AVG(Review Rating)`
- **Granularity:** category, season

## 8. Assumptions, Constraints, and Open Questions

### 8.1 Assumptions
- The BI solution will be built on a DWH and consumed through Power BI.
- Weekly batch loads are sufficient for business needs.
- No cloud infrastructure is required.
- The analysts will use the solution primarily for weekly operational analysis and ad-hoc exploration.

### 8.2 Constraints
- Only the three provided CSV files are in scope.
- The solution must not depend on cloud infrastructure.
- The user base is limited to 5 analysts.
- The solution must support business users rather than software engineers.

### 8.3 Open Questions Requiring Future Clarification
1. **Customer geography granularity:** The business requested analysis by US states, but the inspected customer source contains a `Location` field rather than an explicitly confirmed state field. It is not yet clear whether `Location` represents US state, city, or another geography level.
2. **Age group definition:** The business requested analysis by age groups, but no age banding rules were provided. The grouping logic must be defined later.
3. **Product conversion mapping:** The conversion rate depends on interaction events of type `view` and `purchase`, but the exact relationship between `user id`, `product id`, and the customer/product master records should be validated during implementation.
4. **Week definition:** The KPI granularity includes week, but the exact calendar logic for week numbering was not specified.
5. **Category harmonization:** Category appears in both customer and product sources, but it is not yet confirmed whether the values are fully consistent across files.

# Dimensional Model Specification  
**Project:** NovaMart BI Solution for Customer Interactions and Sales Performance Analysis  
**Source:** BRD only  
**Language:** English

## 1. Purpose
This specification defines the dimensional model for NovaMart’s BI solution based exclusively on the BRD. It supports weekly ad-hoc analysis and Power BI reporting for marketing and sales analysts.

## 2. Business Process
The model supports analysis of:
- sales performance,
- customer behavior,
- product interaction trends,
- discount and promo usage,
- review ratings,
- customer purchase frequency segmentation.

## 3. Chosen Schema Type
A **fact constellation (galaxy) schema** is selected because the BRD requires analysis across multiple business processes:
- sales transactions,
- interaction events,
- customer segmentation.

This approach allows shared dimensions such as customer, product, category, season, and time-related analysis where applicable.

## 4. Grain
### 4.1 Fact Sales
One row per sales transaction record from the sales/customer source.

### 4.2 Fact Interaction
One row per interaction event from `E-commerce_sales_data.csv`.

### 4.3 Fact Customer Snapshot
One row per customer for purchase frequency segmentation.

## 5. Facts, Measures, Dimensions, and Hierarchies

### 5.1 Fact Tables

#### 5.1.1 Fact Sales
**Business purpose:** Support revenue, order value, discount, promo, and review analysis.

**Measures:**
- Purchase Amount USD
- Transaction Count
- Discount Applied Count
- Promo Code Used Count
- Review Rating

**Derived KPIs supported:**
- Total Revenue
- Average Order Value
- Discount Rate
- Promo Code Redemption Rate
- Average Review Rating

#### 5.1.2 Fact Interaction
**Business purpose:** Support product conversion analysis based on interaction events.

**Measures:**
- Interaction Event Count
- View Event Count
- Purchase Event Count

**Derived KPI supported:**
- Product Conversion Rate

#### 5.1.3 Fact Customer Snapshot
**Business purpose:** Support customer purchase frequency distribution.

**Measures:**
- Customer Count

**Derived KPI supported:**
- Customer Purchase Frequency Distribution

### 5.2 Dimensions

#### 5.2.1 Dim Customer
Attributes from customer-related source content:
- Customer ID
- Age
- Gender
- Location
- Subscription Status
- Shipping Type
- Previous Purchases
- Payment Method
- Frequency of Purchases
- Age Group

**Notes:**
- Age Group is required by the BRD but no banding rules are provided.
- Location is present in the source, but the BRD requests US state analysis and does not confirm that Location is a state.

#### 5.2.2 Dim Product
Attributes from product-related source content:
- Product ID / Unique ID
- Product Name
- Category
- Selling Price
- Product Url
- Is Amazon Seller
- Additional descriptive product attributes from the source

#### 5.2.3 Dim Category
Attributes:
- Category

#### 5.2.4 Dim Season
Attributes:
- Season

#### 5.2.5 Dim Time
Attributes:
- Time Stamp
- Date
- Week

**Notes:**
- Week definition is not specified in the BRD.

#### 5.2.6 Dim Interaction Type
Attributes:
- Interaction Type

#### 5.2.7 Dim Purchase Frequency
Attributes:
- Frequency of Purchases

### 5.3 Hierarchies
- **Time hierarchy:** Date -> Week
- **Customer segmentation hierarchy:** Age -> Age Group
- **Customer behavior segmentation:** Frequency of Purchases
- **Product hierarchy:** Category
- **Commercial analysis hierarchy:** Season

## 6. Open Questions
The following items are explicitly ambiguous or incomplete in the BRD and must be confirmed later:
1. **Customer geography granularity:** The BRD requests analysis by US state, but the source contains `Location` only. It is not confirmed whether this represents state.
2. **Age group definition:** No age banding rules are provided.
3. **Week definition:** The calendar logic for week numbering is not specified.
4. **Product conversion mapping:** The exact mapping between `user id`, `product id`, customer records, and product records requires validation.
5. **Category harmonization:** Category appears in multiple sources, but consistency across files is not confirmed.
6. **Fact Sales grain:** The BRD does not explicitly define a transaction identifier in the source description.
7. **Fact Customer Snapshot grain:** The BRD requests distribution by frequency of purchases, but the snapshot timing is not specified.

## 7. Source-to-Model Mapping

### 7.1 customer_details.csv
Mapped to:
- Dim Customer
- Fact Sales

### 7.2 E-commerce_sales_data.csv
Mapped to:
- Fact Interaction
- Dim Time
- Dim Interaction Type

### 7.3 product_details.csv
Mapped to:
- Dim Product
- Dim Category

## 8. Business Rules
- Weekly batch refresh is required.
- KPI definitions must follow the BRD exactly.
- No cloud infrastructure is in scope.
- Only the three provided CSV sources are in scope.
- The model must be suitable for Power BI consumption.

## 9. KPI Definitions
### 9.1 Total Revenue
`SUM(Purchase Amount USD)`

### 9.2 Average Order Value
`Total Revenue / number of transactions`

### 9.3 Discount Rate
`COUNT(transactions where Discount Applied = Yes) / COUNT(all transactions)`

### 9.4 Promo Code Redemption Rate
`COUNT(transactions where Promo Code Used = Yes) / COUNT(all transactions)`

### 9.5 Product Conversion Rate
`COUNT(interaction events where type = purchase) / COUNT(interaction events where type = view)`

### 9.6 Customer Purchase Frequency Distribution
Distribution of customers grouped by `Frequency of Purchases`

### 9.7 Average Review Rating
`AVG(Review Rating)`

## 10. Analytical Requirements Coverage
- Revenue by category, season, subscription status, week
- Average order value by category, subscription status
- Discount rate by category, season
- Promo code redemption rate by subscription status, season
- Product conversion rate by category
- Customer purchase frequency distribution
- Average review rating by category, season
- Customer analysis by geography, age group, purchase frequency segment

## 11. Conclusion
The recommended dimensional model is a fact constellation with separate facts for sales, interactions, and customer segmentation, supported by shared dimensions for customer, product, category, season, time, and interaction type.
