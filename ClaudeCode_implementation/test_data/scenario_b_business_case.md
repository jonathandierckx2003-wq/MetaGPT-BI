# Scenario B: Business Case — PrimeShelf Multi-Vendor Marketplace Analytics
## MetaGPT-BI Scenario B: Airbyte Cloud (Faker API) → Supabase (PostgreSQL) Warehouse

*PoC scenario for the thesis "Where AI Adds Value: Designing a BI development Multi-Agent Architecture" — Jonathan Dierckx, UNamur / KU Leuven, 2026.*

---

## The company

**PrimeShelf** is a cloud-native multi-vendor marketplace operating several specialised online storefronts (e.g. PrimeShelf Home, PrimeShelf Office, PrimeShelf Tech). Vendors list their products under a storefront, and customers — a mix of individual consumers and small businesses — shop across storefronts in a single checkout flow.

PrimeShelf's operational platform is fully cloud-hosted and exposes transactional data through a managed API integration layer (Airbyte Cloud). The commercial analytics team of 4 currently exports data manually from the platform's admin portal and pastes it into spreadsheets — a fragile process that loses historical data each time a new export overwrites the previous one.

---

## The data

Three live data streams are available via the Airbyte Cloud "Sample Data (Faker)" connector already configured in PrimeShelf's Airbyte workspace:

| Stream | What it contains |
|--------|-----------------|
| `users` | One row per registered customer — customer ID, name, email, gender, age, company (for B2B accounts) |
| `products` | One row per product listing — product ID, name, description, store name (the storefront it belongs to), list price |
| `purchases` | One row per completed purchase — purchase ID, customer ID, product ID, quantity, revenue, store name, purchase date |

The `customer_id` in `purchases` matches the `id` in `users`. The `product_id` in `purchases` matches the `id` in `products`.

---

## What PrimeShelf needs

A cloud Data Warehouse (Supabase) that the analytics team can query directly with SQL — no more manual exports — to answer the following questions:

- Which storefronts and products generate the most revenue, and how does this evolve over time?
- What is the revenue contribution of B2B customers (those with a non-null `company` field) versus individual consumers?
- Which customers are the top buyers by total spend and by number of orders?
- How are purchases distributed across age groups and genders?
- What is the average order quantity and revenue per product, and which products are ordered most frequently?

**Preferred DWH technology:** Supabase (cloud PostgreSQL — no local infrastructure, data must remain cloud-accessible).  
**Data ingestion:** Airbyte Cloud (existing workspace; Faker source already created).  
**Refresh frequency:** Daily Airbyte syncs (full-refresh-overwrite).

## Key KPIs

| KPI | Formula | Granularity |
|-----|---------|-------------|
| Total Revenue | SUM(revenue) | By store, product, customer, date (year / month / day) |
| Average Order Value | SUM(revenue) / COUNT(purchase_id) | By store, customer segment (B2B vs. consumer) |
| Average Order Quantity | SUM(quantity) / COUNT(purchase_id) | By product, store |
| B2B Revenue Share | SUM(revenue WHERE company IS NOT NULL) / SUM(revenue) | Overall and by store |
| Top Customers by Revenue | SUM(revenue) ranked | Top-N per store or overall |
| Purchase Frequency per Customer | COUNT(purchase_id) per customer_id | Distribution across customer base |
| Revenue Trend | SUM(revenue) | By year, month, day of week |
